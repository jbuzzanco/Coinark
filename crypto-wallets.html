Mobile Wallets

Blue Wallet is advertised as a radically simple and powerful bitcoin wallet. It is a BITCOIN ONLY wallet. Do not plan to use this wallet for other cryptocurrencies. They have a mobile app for Apple and Android as well as a desktop version Check out the official website for more information and an explanation of all of their features (bluewallet.io). Contact us if you have questions about Blue Wallet or how to use a wallet with Coinark ATMs.

Blue Wallet's official website
Blue wallet can be downloaded free through the App Store on iPhone or Google Play on Android.

Cold Wallets

Trezor
Trezor Model T: This is Trezor’s premium cold wallet that costs around $215. Trezor provides a great UI experience and is extremely easy to use. Supports almost all cryptocurrencies.


Trezor One: This is Trezor's affordable option that costs around $70. It has a Monochrome display with 2 buttons. It's a simple/basic cold wallet but it gets the job done. This wallet supports over 1000 cryptocurrencies.
Trezor's official website.

Ledger
Ledger Nano X: The Nano X is the ledger's premium cold wallet that costs about $150. Ledger provides a good UI experience along with a mobile app that pairs with their software so you can use it on the go. They support over 5,500 cryptocurrencies and support over 100 cryptos on the Nano X at one time. It's one screen and 2 button design.

Ledger Nano S: The Nano S is their budget version that costs around $60. There is not much difference in design or features other than it supports considerably fewer cryptos at one time. 5 to 6 cryptocurrencies can be stored at one time. You aren't able to store a lot but it's good value for the price.
Ledger Nano S Pro: Ledger is working on a new cold wallet. It is currently in the works and the release date is unknown. If you visit their website you can sign up and they will send you information about it.

Ledger's official website.
